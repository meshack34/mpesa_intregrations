LEAD Projects

1.0 Capacity Building

LEAD facilitates knowledge and skills acquisition for rural communities, enabling effective participation in livelihood projects. This is achieved through periodical training for farmer groups. Our team comprises experienced specialists in agriculture, livestock, and community development, dedicated to capacity building.

2.0 Livelihood Projects/TIMPs

LEAD encourages investment in income-generating activities to improve living standards through innovative technologies, targeting vulnerable groups with support in inputs and materials.

A) Dairy Production (10 Litre Milk Project)

Supports small-scale dairy farmers with training in pasture management, housing, feeding, disease control, breeding, and marketing.

B) Indigenous Chicken Production

Focuses on sourcing improved chicks, brooding, feed formulation, hatching, and disease management.

C) Horticulture

Promotes high-value crops such as avocados, passion fruits, carrots, and beetroots.

D) African Leafy Vegetables (ALV)

Encourages organic production of vegetables like amaranths, spider plants, pumpkins, and black nightshade.

E) Dairy Goat Production

Ideal for smallholder farmers, focusing on housing, breeding, feeding, and disease control.
